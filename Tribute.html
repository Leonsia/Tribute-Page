Hedy Lamarr

Tribute page of a beautiful woman, bold actress and smart inventor.

Awesome facts:

  • Heddy got a title of the "most beautiful woman in Europe" from a film director Max Reinhardt, shared by audiences and critics.
  • Just accompaning her husband at business meetings with scientists involved Hedy in the field of apply science.
  • Lamarr's earliest inventions included an improved traffic stoplight and a tablet that would dissolve in water to create a carbonated drink.

    • "Hope and curiosity about the future seemed better than guarantees. That's the way I was. The unknown was always so attractive to me... and still is."

  • Beginning of the II World War inspired Lamarr to design a jam-proof radio guidance system for torpedoes, to prevent enemies from being able to detect the messages by changing radio frequencies simultaneously.
  • The co-inventor of a “Secret Communication System” was a composer George Antheil. Heddy proposed to use the principle of piano rolls to change among 88 frequencies.
  • The invented principles of wireless security are now incorporated into modern Wi-Fi, GSM and Bluetooth technologies.
  • Hedy Lamarr has got a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame at 6247 Hollywood Blvd.

Biography:

  • 1914 - Hedwig Eva Maria Kiesler was born in Vienna.
  • 1933 - Lamarr married an austrian millionaire Friedrich Mandl. First starring in Gustav Machaty's film "Ecstasy".
  • 1937 - Escaped to Paris, where she met film producer and co-founder of Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer studios Louis B. Mayer.
  • 1938 - First Hollywood film debute in "Algiers", created a national sensation due to the beauty of Hedy Lamarr.
  • 1942 - The new “Secret Communication System” technology designed by Hedy Lamarr and composer George Antheil was granted a patent.
  • 1962 - Updated version of the technology design was implemented on U.S. Navy Ships.
  • 1997 - Hedy Lamarr and George Antheil received the Electronic Frontier Foundation Pioneer Award, given to individuals who have made significant contributions to the empowerment of individuals in using computers.
  • 2000 - Hedy Lamarr died at the age of 85 in Florida.
  • 2014 - Lamarr and Antheil were posthumously inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame.
